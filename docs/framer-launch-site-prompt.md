# Copy-ready Framer website prompt

Build a polished, responsive, one-page marketing website for **NewGen Automation**, a workflow platform designed for New Zealand family law practices.

The site must feel calm, credible and operational. It is legal-practice software, not an AI product. Use plain New Zealand English. Do not use the phrases “AI-powered”, “revolutionary”, “disruptive” or “replace your staff”. Do not invent testimonials, law-firm logos, customer numbers, usage statistics, certifications, awards, compliance claims, pricing or savings percentages.

## Brand and visual direction

- Product name: **NewGen Automation**.
- Palette: white, restrained slate and a clear sky-blue accent.
- Typography: a modern, highly legible sans serif. Use a restrained type scale and generous line height.
- Style: professional SaaS interface, fine grey borders, soft shadows used sparingly, compact status badges and strong spacing rhythm.
- Avoid gradients, decorative blobs, oversized illustrations, stock photos, courtroom clichés, gavels and scales of justice.
- Keep the content width controlled and the navigation simple.
- Meet accessible contrast standards, include obvious keyboard focus states, use semantic headings and ensure every form control has a visible label.
- Design mobile-first, with clean tablet and desktop layouts. Tables and screenshot galleries must remain usable on narrow screens.

## Header

Create a sticky white header with a subtle bottom border.

- Left: NewGen Automation wordmark in text.
- Navigation anchors: Workflow, Features, Product, Security, Integrations.
- Primary button: **Book a demonstration**.
- On mobile, use a compact accessible menu and keep the demonstration button prominent.

## Workflow section

Heading: **From intake to follow-up, without losing the thread.**

Show a six-step horizontal sequence on desktop and a vertical sequence on mobile:

1. Intake — Capture applicant, respondent, relationship, children, court and application details once.
2. Matter documents — Generate the required Family Court matter bundle from the saved intake.
3. Legal Aid — See missing evidence, prepare the application and record when it has been submitted.
4. Billing — Select completed work and calculate fees, GST, totals, wording and Word placeholders.
5. Claims — Track generated, sent, paid, part-paid and overdue Legal Aid claims.
6. Storage — Download locally or explicitly save to a configured firm storage provider.

Use small numbered cards, not illustrations.

## Feature sections

Create five alternating feature sections. Each section must pair concise copy with one supplied product screenshot.

### Matter generation

Heading: **Build the matter bundle from one structured intake.**

Copy: “Enter the matter information once, including every child affected by the application. NewGen prepares the required document bundle and makes each document’s status clear: Generated, Not Required or Awaiting Requirements.”

Bullets:

- Protection and Parenting documents appear only when required.
- Additional children are carried into styled continuation sections rather than omitted.
- Family Violence Affidavits remain editable for the lawyer’s final review.

Use the New Matter and Generated Matter Bundle screenshots.

### Legal Aid

Heading: **Make the next Legal Aid action obvious.**

Copy: “See whether income proof or signed page 5 is missing, which applications are ready to generate, and what has been generated or submitted.”

Bullets:

- Required uploads sit beside the current application state.
- Matter details flow through from intake for review.
- Submission is recorded as part of the operational queue.

Use the Legal Aid Application screenshot.

### Structured billing

Heading: **Keep lawyers in control of every billing form.**

Copy: “Select the work completed. Fees, GST, totals, wording, and Word placeholders are calculated automatically.”

Bullets:

- Choose Form32B or Form33A and the work actually completed.
- Preview and edit the final wording before generation.
- Download the Word form, optionally create a claim, then choose storage separately.

Use the Structured Billing screenshot.

### Claims tracking

Heading: **Know what was claimed, paid and still needs follow-up.**

Copy: “A spreadsheet-like claims tracker records each claim from Draft or Generated through Sent and Paid, while highlighting overdue and part-paid balances.”

Bullets:

- Search by client, matter, Legal Aid number or claim ID.
- Record sent, paid and partial-payment dates and amounts.
- See weekly, monthly, outstanding and overdue totals at a glance.

Use the Claims Tracker screenshot.

### Cloud storage

Heading: **Storage remains an explicit firm choice.**

Copy: “Lawyers can download documents to their computer or save them to a storage provider configured for their practice. Storage actions are visible and deliberate.”

Bullets:

- Microsoft Word documents remain available for lawyer review.
- Show OneDrive actions only where OneDrive has been configured.
- Mention Google Drive only conditionally: it is available only where a working Google Drive adapter has been configured.

Use the Generated Matter Bundle screenshot or a close crop of its storage actions.

## Security section

Heading: **Designed for firm-controlled information.**

Use careful, factual copy:

“NewGen Automation is configured for each practice. Matter records are separated by firm, and storage actions use the integrations configured for that firm. Access, hosting, database and storage settings remain part of the practice’s implementation and operating controls.”

Add three restrained cards:

- Firm-scoped records — Claims and operational records include a firm identifier so one firm’s records are not presented to another firm.
- Configured integrations — Storage options appear only when the relevant provider has been configured.
- Deliberate document handling — Download and cloud-storage actions are separate, visible user choices.

Do not claim certification, legal compliance, data residency, zero access, end-to-end encryption, independent audit or a particular security standard unless those claims are supplied later and verified.

## Integrations

Heading: **Works with the tools around the matter.**

Show simple text-and-icon cards for:

- Microsoft Word — Generated legal and billing documents remain editable for review.
- Microsoft OneDrive — Optional firm storage when configured.
- Google Drive — Show as “Available with a configured Google Drive adapter”; do not imply it is enabled by default.

Do not add Adobe, accounting, payment or practice-management integrations.
